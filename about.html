Who I Am

Hey, I’m Meshack Matheka, widely known as Makamesco. I’m a Kenyan developer, teacher trainee, and self-driven technologist with a relentless hunger to learn, build, and solve real problems. What started as curiosity and trial-and-error has turned into a journey of continuous growth—one new skill every month, one project at a time.

I bridge education and technology. My training as a teacher fuels my empathy for users and learners, while my passion for development pushes me to build tools, websites, and systems that are not only functional but meaningful. I believe that tech should empower communities, simplify complexity, and create opportunities where none existed before.

Background

I am currently a teacher trainee in Kenya, and alongside that path, I’ve immersed myself in the tech ecosystem—from building static HTML/CSS sites to architecting more dynamic applications. I’ve collaborated on several projects, both independently and with others, tackling real-world challenges in digital presence, automation, and user experience.

My journey hasn’t been about waiting for the perfect moment; it’s been about showing up, experimenting, failing, iterating, and shipping. I constantly remix knowledge from design, development, and education to create solutions that work for people, especially those in environments where resources are limited but ambition is high.

Values & Approach

  • Curiosity: I learn something new every month—whether it’s a framework, a language, or a workflow improvement.
  • Impact: I prioritize projects that help others—students, creators, small businesses, and communities.
  • Simplicity: I turn complexity into intuitive experiences. If it can be made easier to understand or use, I’ll refine it.
  • Ownership: I build, ship, and support. If it’s my project, I’m in it from idea to deployment.
  • Collaboration: I value learning with others, sharing openly, and lifting up the people I work with.
